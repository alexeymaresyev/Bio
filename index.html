• Beginning of the book is somewhat immersive, leaving me to believe that there’s a possibility that it will become lame around the middle-end proportion. Instead the furry bear goes towards the human in the snow, the snow crunching (as the author stated). That man was Alexey Maresyev. Luck was on his side…. This time.

• Encountered severe pain during his time of survival, amputated legs only allowed him to travel 10-5 kilometers max. Thus, due to starvation, he eat massive amounts of snow in a animal-like manner that would frighten anyone. • A fox, a “Bobik” dog, a hedgehog, and other insects/animals were encountered. During the fox encounter, Petrovich states that it is said that when animals follow you, that means death is close. He kept an optimistic mind throughout his amputated quest for survival, not only breaking his toes, but his nails, meaning that at one point he had to start crawling with his shoulders, which was an “awkward” experience according to Petrovich.

• Petrovich survived 11 days out in the freezing wild--spend 7 days recovering in a Russian home.
